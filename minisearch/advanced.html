And now before continuing let me explain you something.
When I do a simple search and you ask me to search based on a page say http://www.sitename.com/dir/subdir/file.html,
I search all pages that start with "http://www.sitename.com". But you may want a more narrow search, for example
you may want to search only below http://www.sitename.com/dir - only those pages that start with "http://www.sitename.com/dir".
In this case that is what you should type in the next field: http://www.sitename.com/dir. Of course you should fill in your path -
- not the one from the example (which doesn't even exist)

When I do my search I start at a single page. I follow the links on that page, then explore the pages
those links refer to and follow the links on those pages and so on and so forth. The page I start with
is called a seed page. It can be any page, but it better be the page that will refer me to more pages within your search path.
